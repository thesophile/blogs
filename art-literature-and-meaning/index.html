Art, Literature and Meaning

June 7, 2026

What is poetry? When I was little, I thought it was just a little dance with pretty poetic words - Similies, Metaphors, beutiful descriptions… In a sense thats true, It is poetic to compare grief with a gloomy sky. But comparing object A to Object B is not the part which makes it a poem. Lets look at some examples of poetic phrases/sentence:

  1. A fallen flower compared to the impermanence of beauty.1
  2. Season-emotions: example - summer to Happy, winter to grief etc.
  3. Daily life comparisons - “My head is exploding”, “I am drowning in work” “A weight lifted off my shoulders.” “The color drained from my world.”

We use these so often in out lives so that we have to keep saying “literally” when the words really mean what they are saying. Because for us humans, words are not just a way to communicate physical states or feelings but also emotional ones. They help to communicate what we are feeling. That is why poetry often comes around in our daily lives, even without us noticing.

From these we can see poetry is not just comparison/description of objects, that is just a method for something else. The goal is to evoke emotions in others. It is a good poem if it evokes the desired emotions.

But wait, does that mean anything that evokes emotion is a poem? Then any death would be a poem so would be a hug from a loved one. Even though they are beautiful (the hug I mean) I would simply call them just emotions, however good or bad they are. So we must also consider intention? If they wanted you to be happy, and then just laugh uncontrollably or show you a video of a disaster you may feel certain emotions. Is that art? If that is the case, a chef wanting you to feel sad after eating a food would be as good as an artist who want you to feel happy after the food. A cringe youtuber who is cringe, if he wanted to invike cringeness in you, and succeeds would also be a good artist?

According to M.N Vijayan, a poem is what that has more than one meaning.

“Something that does have a meaning is not a poem, it is what is beyond meaning. … The word “pen” doesn’t usually evoke any emotions in us. But a quill is different. It is pluck from a bird. Kings used to have them on their crowns as decorative objects. Taking a quill from a bird hurts. As Vailopilli said, What sprouts from quills are Live breathing blood.”

– M N Vijayante Samboorna Krithikal Volume 2

The point is it can evoke a wide range of emotions

…….

I refine this further: “A poem is something that has two meanings: A literal meaning and an emotional meaning which is more than the sum of the parts”

Here the important distinction is “more than the some of the parts”. Cause some statements/factual or literal descriptions can have both but in those cases the emotional meaning comes directly from the literal meaning, one invoking another.

Contrary to this, some beutiful poems make you feel for example sad without using any word/sentence that makes you sad outside it’s context.

So context is important here. Thats why different people have different interpretations for the same part.

This definition still applies to All arts in general, just replace “literal meaning”, with “Elemental meaning” or perhaps just “literal meaning” itself if used figuratively.

“An Art is something that has two meanings: An Elemental meaning and an Emotional meaning which is more than the sum of the elemental meaning.”

For music, individual notes are the elements, they dont say much except higher notes have a slightly different “feeling” than lower notes. But when combined in a beutiful way, the whole suddenly gains emotional meaning, more than sum of the parts. That IS Art.

Same with Movies2, paintings and every Art.

Art doesn’t have to make you feel good or provoke thought or give you a message - sometimes creators force to put these in their works and that makes it less of an Art.

……

Anyway, thats just my personal definition of Art and I judge Art with this internal model.

  2. Movies are a good example for our case. We can see a footage of a person getting a job and feel nothing. But a good movie can make you cry with emotional overwhelm after watching the same thing. refer 12th Fail ↩︎
